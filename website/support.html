Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the turbulence forecasts?

Turby uses real-time aviation weather data from NOAA (METARs, TAFs, SIGMETs, PIREPs) and global weather models to estimate turbulence. While our forecasts give a strong indication of conditions, weather is inherently unpredictable. Turby is for informational purposes — always follow crew instructions during your flight.

What data sources does Turby use?

We pull from NOAA's Aviation Weather Center (METARs, TAFs, SIGMETs, PIREPs, G-AIRMETs), Open-Meteo for upper-atmosphere wind data along your route, and AeroDataBox for real-time flight status and schedules. All sources are updated continuously.

How do I search for my flight?

You can search by flight number (e.g. AA1234) or by route (e.g. JFK to LHR). When searching by flight number, Turby will show all flights for that number on your selected date with departure times, so you can pick the right one.

What does the turbulence score mean?

The score ranges from 0 to 10. 0–2 means smooth conditions, 3–4 is light turbulence (minor bumps), 5–7 is moderate (seatbelt recommended), and 8–10 is severe (rare, significant bumps). Most flights score between 1 and 4.

What is Calm Mode?

Calm Mode is designed for nervous flyers. It provides breathing exercises, reassuring turbulence facts, and gentle guidance to help you feel more relaxed before and during your flight.

How do I manage or cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed through Apple. Go to Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions on your iPhone, find Turby, and tap to manage or cancel. You can also manage subscriptions at apps.apple.com/account/subscriptions.

How do I restore my purchases?

Open Turby, go to Settings > Subscription, and tap Restore Purchases. This will restore any active subscription tied to your Apple ID. Make sure you're signed in with the same Apple ID you used to subscribe.

How do I request a refund?

Since all purchases go through Apple, refund requests must be submitted directly to Apple. Visit Apple's support page to request a refund for any in-app purchase or subscription.

Can pilots use Turby for flight planning?

Turby includes a "Pilot Mode" that shows additional technical data. However, Turby is not a certified aviation tool and should never replace official weather briefings, dispatch systems, or pilot decision-making tools. It's meant as a supplementary informational resource.

My flight isn't showing up. What should I do?

Make sure you're entering the correct airline code and flight number (e.g. UA910, not United 910). Also check that the date is correct — Turby only shows flights for the selected date. If it's a very small regional carrier, it may not be in our database.

Does Turby track my location?

No. Turby does not request or use your location. All data is based on the flight information you search for.

Does Turby work offline?

Turby requires an internet connection to fetch real-time weather and flight data. However, your previously searched flights are saved locally and can be viewed offline.

Subscription Information

Turby offers a free tier and premium subscriptions:

  • Weekly — auto-renews every 7 days
  • Monthly — auto-renews every month
  • Yearly — auto-renews every 12 months (best value)

Payment is charged to your Apple ID account. Subscriptions auto-renew unless canceled at least 24 hours before the end of the current period. Manage subscriptions in your Apple ID settings.
